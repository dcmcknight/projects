Executive Summary:¶

A style of animation originating from Japan, "Anime" has become increasingly popular in the West in the past couple decades. The purpose of this memo is to perform an exploratory data analysis of an anime dataset to find compelling relationships between the variables and to create a model using KNN to see how accurately it can predict the similarities in popularity (overall rating) of different anime based on genre, source material, and number of members (people who are interested in it, not necessarily watching it currently) following the anime.

Below are the questions I chose to answer for my exploratory analysis:

  • outliers, missing values, descriptive stats
  • Top rated animes
  • Anime with most eps
  • Anime rating trends over time
  • Top animes based on membership following
  • Top genres based on rating
  • genres with the most animes tagged to it
  • relationship between source material and rating

In my findings, I found that anime as a whole has enjoyed a meteoric rise in popularity over the past several decades. The year 2000 marks when ratings started displaying a more consistent increase, almost 10 years. Ratings endured a slight dip around 2007-2008, but then rose again around 2010-2015. This may be primarily due to expansion of the internet and globalism in general which allowed wider foreign audiences to consume anime from Japan. A large number of anime had rating of zero, which indicated the anime not having a rating at all, so I omitted those rows from this analysis to not skew the data.

Interestingly, comedy-based anime made up the vast majority of the data (~11%) based on number of anime (4275). I was also surprised to discover that Action and Fantasy anime did not make the top 10 in terms of average rating even though they make up ~8% and ~5%, respectively, of the data. Source material for a given anime was another interesting feature to explore. Manga based anime made up the majority of anime and had one the highest median ratings.

I created two models (regression and classification) for this dataset to compare the accuracy. I chose the member total, source material, and genre as predictors based on the assumption of that these factors would be the strongest indicators for similarities in popularity. I considered episode total as a predictor, but my analysis showed that volume of episodes is not necessarily a good indicator of similarities in popularity. Oyako Club, which is a kids anime, has almost 1800 episodes but does not crack the top 20 in terms of overall rating. The classification model proved to be more accurate by ~10%. To increase accuracy for either model, more data would need to be added to this dataset to offset curse of dimensionality.

Feature Engineering:¶

  • dropped rows with a zero rating
  • created dummies for source material for KNN algorithm
  • 'year' based on anime air date

Data Dictionary:¶

  • anime_id - identifier for anime title
  • anime_name - title of the anime
  • studio_id - identifier for studio that created the anime
  • studio_name - name of studio
  • episodes_total - total number of episodes for the anime
  • source_material - source from which the anime originated before adapted into a TV show. Originals, Manga, Video game, book, light novel, etc.
  • air_date - date the anime started running
  • overall_rating - rating given to the anime based on viewers feedback
  • members - number of people following the title (not necessarily watching it)
  • synopsis - plot of the anime
  • number of tags - number of genres that are tagged to the anime
  • Genres:
    • 'Action', 'Adventure', 'Cars', 'Comedy', 'Dementia', 'Demons', 'Drama', 'Ecchi', 'Fantasy', 'Game', 'Harem', 'Hentai', 'Historical', 'Horror', 'Josei', 'Kids', 'Magic', 'Martial_Arts', 'Mecha', 'Military', 'Music', 'Mystery', 'Parody', 'Police', 'Psychological', 'Romance', 'Samurai', 'School', 'Sci-Fi', 'Seinen', 'Shoujo', 'Shoujo_Ai', 'Shounen', 'Shounen_Ai', 'Slice_of_Life', 'Space', 'Sports', 'Super_Power', 'Supernatural', 'Thriller', 'Vampire', 'Yaoi', 'Yuri'
    • The above genres are indicated by 0 or 1. 0 = anime is not associated with this genre, 1 = anime is associated with this genre!
